Chapter 5Rotational Motion and Rigid Bodies

5.1 Angular Kinematics

Definition 5.1 — Angular Quantities

Angular displacement $\theta$, angular velocity $\omega = d\theta/dt$, angular acceleration $\alpha = d\omega/dt$. For constant $\alpha$:

$$\omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t, \qquad \theta = \omega_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2}\alpha t^2, \qquad \omega^2 = \omega_0^2 + 2\alpha\theta.$$

5.2 Torque and Moment of Inertia

Definition 5.2 — Torque
$$\boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{F}, \qquad |\tau| = rF\sin\phi = Fd$$

where $d$ is the moment arm (perpendicular distance from the axis to the line of action of $\mathbf{F}$).

Definition 5.3 — Moment of Inertia
$$I = \sum_i m_i r_i^2 = \int r^2\,dm$$

where $r$ is the perpendicular distance from the rotation axis.

Theorem 5.1 — Parallel Axis Theorem
$$I = I_{\text{cm}} + Md^2$$

where $d$ is the distance between the two parallel axes.

Theorem 5.2 — Perpendicular Axis Theorem (Planar bodies)
$$I_z = I_x + I_y$$

where $z$ is perpendicular to the plane containing the body.

5.3 Newton's Second Law for Rotation

Theorem 5.3
$$\tau_{\text{net}} = I\alpha \qquad\text{(fixed axis)}$$

5.4 Angular Momentum

Definition 5.4 — Angular Momentum
$$\mathbf{L} = \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{p} = I\boldsymbol{\omega} \qquad\text{(rigid body, fixed axis)}.$$
Theorem 5.4 — Conservation of Angular Momentum

If $\tau_{\text{ext}} = 0$, then $L = I\omega = \text{const}$.

Example 5.1 — Figure Skater

A skater with arms extended has $I_1 = 4.0\,\text{kg·m}^2$ and spins at $\omega_1 = 2\pi\,\text{rad/s}$. She pulls her arms in, reducing $I$ to $I_2 = 1.5\,\text{kg·m}^2$. Find her new angular velocity.

Solution

$I_1\omega_1 = I_2\omega_2 \implies \omega_2 = (4.0/1.5)(2\pi) \approx 16.8\,\text{rad/s} \approx 2.7\,\text{rev/s}$.

5.5 Rolling Without Slipping

Theorem 5.5 — Rolling Constraint

For a body of radius $R$ rolling without slipping: $v_{\text{cm}} = R\omega$ and $a_{\text{cm}} = R\alpha$. The contact point has zero instantaneous velocity.

Example 5.2 — Sphere Rolling Down an Incline

A solid sphere rolls without slipping down a plane of angle $\theta$. Find the acceleration.

Solution

Translation: $mg\sin\theta - f = ma$. Rotation: $fR = I\alpha = \frac{2}{5}mR^2 \cdot (a/R)$, so $f = \frac{2}{5}ma$. Substituting: $mg\sin\theta = ma + \frac{2}{5}ma = \frac{7}{5}ma$. Thus $a = \frac{5}{7}g\sin\theta$.

5.6 Rotational Kinetic Energy

$$K_{\text{rot}} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2, \qquad K_{\text{total}} = \frac{1}{2}Mv_{\text{cm}}^2 + \frac{1}{2}I_{\text{cm}}\omega^2.$$

5.7 Exercises

Exercise 5.1

A uniform disk of mass $M$ and radius $R$ can rotate freely about a horizontal axis through its center. A mass $m$ is attached at the rim. Released from rest with $m$ at the 3 o'clock position, find the angular velocity when $m$ reaches the bottom.

Exercise 5.2

A rod of length $L$ and mass $M$ is pivoted at one end and released from horizontal. Find the angular velocity and the reaction force at the pivot when the rod is vertical.

Exercise 5.3

A solid cylinder and a hollow cylinder of the same mass and radius race down an incline from the same height. Which arrives first and by how much time?
