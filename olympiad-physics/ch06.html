Chapter 6Fluid Mechanics

6.1 Pressure and Pascal's Principle

Definition 6.1 — Pressure
$$P = \frac{dF}{dA}$$

Pressure is a scalar; force on a surface element $d\mathbf{A}$ is $d\mathbf{F} = -P\,d\mathbf{A}$ (pointing inward).

Pascal's Principle

A change in pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished to every point of the fluid and to the walls of the container.

6.2 Hydrostatic Pressure

Theorem 6.1 — Hydrostatic Equation
$$\frac{dP}{dz} = -\rho g \qquad\implies\qquad P = P_0 + \rho g h$$

for an incompressible fluid of density $\rho$ at depth $h$ below the surface.

6.3 Archimedes' Principle

Theorem 6.2 — Archimedes' Principle

The buoyant force on a submerged (or partially submerged) body equals the weight of the displaced fluid:

$$F_b = \rho_f V_{\text{disp}}\,g.$$
Example 6.1 — Floating Body

A block of density $\rho_s$ and volume $V$ floats in a liquid of density $\rho_\ell > \rho_s$. What fraction is submerged?

Solution

Equilibrium: $\rho_s V g = \rho_\ell V_{\text{sub}} g$. Fraction submerged $= V_{\text{sub}}/V = \rho_s/\rho_\ell$.

6.4 Continuity Equation

Theorem 6.3 — Equation of Continuity

For an incompressible fluid in steady flow: $A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2$.

6.5 Bernoulli's Equation

Theorem 6.4 — Bernoulli's Equation

For steady, incompressible, non-viscous flow along a streamline:

$$P + \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{const}.$$
Proof

Apply the work–energy theorem to a fluid element flowing through a tube. The work done by pressure forces and gravity equals the change in kinetic energy. Consider a section of streamtube between points 1 and 2. In time $dt$, a volume $A_1 v_1\,dt = A_2 v_2\,dt = dV$ flows through.

Net work by pressure: $(P_1 - P_2)dV$. Work by gravity: $-\rho g(h_2 - h_1)dV$. Change in KE: $\frac{1}{2}\rho(v_2^2 - v_1^2)dV$.

Combining: $P_1 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_1^2 + \rho g h_1 = P_2 + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 + \rho g h_2$. $\blacksquare$

Example 6.2 — Torricelli's Theorem

A tank of water filled to height $H$ has a small hole at the bottom. Find the exit speed.

Solution

Apply Bernoulli between the top surface (large area, $v \approx 0$, $P = P_{\text{atm}}$, $h = H$) and the hole ($P = P_{\text{atm}}$, $h = 0$):

$P_{\text{atm}} + \rho g H = P_{\text{atm}} + \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2 \implies v = \sqrt{2gH}$.

6.6 Viscous Flow and Stokes' Law

For a sphere of radius $r$ moving at speed $v$ through a fluid of viscosity $\eta$:

$$F_{\text{drag}} = 6\pi\eta r v \qquad\text{(Stokes' law, low Reynolds number)}.$$

Terminal velocity of a falling sphere: $v_t = \frac{2r^2(\rho_s - \rho_f)g}{9\eta}$.

6.7 Surface Tension

Definition 6.2 — Surface Tension

Surface tension $\gamma$ is the force per unit length along a line on the surface, or equivalently the energy per unit area. Excess pressure inside a bubble of radius $R$: $\Delta P = 2\gamma/R$ (single surface) or $4\gamma/R$ (soap bubble, two surfaces).
