Chapter 11Magnetism and Electromagnetic Induction

11.1 Magnetic Force

Lorentz Force Law
$$\mathbf{F} = q(\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B}).$$

The magnetic force does no work (always perpendicular to $\mathbf{v}$).

A charged particle in a uniform $\mathbf{B}$ moves in a circle of radius $r = mv/(qB)$ with cyclotron frequency $\omega_c = qB/m$.

Example 11.1 — Velocity Selector

Crossed $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ fields: a particle passes undeflected when $qE = qvB$, i.e., $v = E/B$.

11.2 Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

$$d\mathbf{F} = I\,d\boldsymbol{\ell}\times\mathbf{B}, \qquad \mathbf{F} = I\mathbf{L}\times\mathbf{B} \;\text{(uniform B)}.$$

11.3 Biot–Savart Law

Theorem 11.1 — Biot–Savart Law
$$d\mathbf{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{I\,d\boldsymbol{\ell}\times\hat{\mathbf{r}}}{r^2}$$

where $\mu_0 = 4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\text{T·m/A}$.

Configuration$B$
Long straight wire (distance $d$)$\mu_0 I/(2\pi d)$
Center of circular loop (radius $R$)$\mu_0 I/(2R)$
Solenoid ($n$ turns/length)$\mu_0 n I$ (inside)
Toroid ($N$ turns, radius $r$)$\mu_0 NI/(2\pi r)$

11.4 Ampère's Law

Theorem 11.2 — Ampère's Law
$$\oint \mathbf{B}\cdot d\boldsymbol{\ell} = \mu_0 I_{\text{enc}}.$$

11.5 Faraday's Law of Induction

Theorem 11.3 — Faraday's Law
$$\mathcal{E} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}, \qquad \Phi_B = \int \mathbf{B}\cdot d\mathbf{A}.$$

Lenz's law: the induced current opposes the change in flux.

Example 11.2 — Rod Sliding on Rails

A conducting rod of length $L$ slides with velocity $v$ on two parallel rails in a uniform field $B$ (perpendicular to the plane of the rails, resistance $R$). Find the induced current and the force needed to maintain constant velocity.

Solution

$\mathcal{E} = BLv$. Current: $I = BLv/R$. Force on the rod (opposing motion): $F = BIL = B^2L^2v/R$. To maintain constant $v$, apply $F = B^2L^2v/R$.

11.6 Inductance

Definition 11.1 — Self-Inductance
$$\mathcal{E}_L = -L\frac{dI}{dt}, \qquad L = \frac{\Phi_B}{I}.$$

Solenoid: $L = \mu_0 n^2 V$ where $V$ is the volume.

Energy stored: $U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$. Energy density: $u = B^2/(2\mu_0)$.

11.7 RL and LC Circuits

RL circuit: $I(t) = (V/R)(1-e^{-t/\tau_L})$ with $\tau_L = L/R$.

LC circuit: $q(t) = Q_0\cos(\omega t + \phi)$ with $\omega = 1/\sqrt{LC}$.
