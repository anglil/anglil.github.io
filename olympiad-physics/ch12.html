Chapter 12Geometric and Wave Optics

12.1 Reflection and Refraction

Law of Reflection
$$\theta_i = \theta_r.$$
Snell's Law of Refraction
$$n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2.$$

Total internal reflection occurs when $\theta_1 > \theta_c = \arcsin(n_2/n_1)$ (for $n_1 > n_2$).

12.2 Mirrors

Mirror Equation
$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}, \qquad f = R/2.$$

Magnification: $m = -d_i/d_o$.

12.3 Thin Lenses

Thin Lens Equation
$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}$$

with sign conventions: real images/objects positive on the appropriate side.

Lensmaker's Equation
$$\frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right).$$
Example 12.1 — Compound Lens System

Two thin lenses of focal lengths $f_1$ and $f_2$ are separated by distance $d$. Find the image of an object at infinity.

Solution

The first lens produces an image at $f_1$. This serves as an object for the second lens at distance $d_o' = d - f_1$. Using the lens equation for the second lens: $1/d_i' = 1/f_2 - 1/(d-f_1)$.

12.4 Wave Optics: Interference

Theorem 12.1 — Young's Double Slit

For slits separated by $d$, screen at distance $L$, wavelength $\lambda$:

$$d\sin\theta = m\lambda \;\text{(bright)}, \qquad d\sin\theta = (m+\tfrac{1}{2})\lambda \;\text{(dark)}.$$

Fringe spacing: $\Delta y = \lambda L/d$.

12.5 Thin-Film Interference

For a film of thickness $t$ and index $n$, the path difference is $2nt$. Account for phase shifts of $\pi$ (half-wavelength) at each reflection from a higher-$n$ medium.

Example 12.2 — Anti-Reflection Coating

Find the minimum thickness of a coating (index $n_c$) on glass (index $n_g > n_c$) for destructive reflection at wavelength $\lambda$.

Solution

Both reflections (air→coating, coating→glass) involve going to a higher-$n$ medium, so both get a $\pi$ phase shift — they cancel. Destructive interference requires $2n_c t = \lambda/2$, so $t = \lambda/(4n_c)$.

12.6 Diffraction

Single-Slit Diffraction

For a slit of width $a$: $a\sin\theta = m\lambda$ gives minima ($m = \pm1, \pm2, \ldots$). Central maximum angular width: $2\lambda/a$.

Diffraction Grating

$N$ slits with spacing $d$: maxima at $d\sin\theta = m\lambda$. Angular width of each peak $\propto 1/(Nd)$. Resolving power: $R = mN$.

12.7 Polarization

Malus's law: $I = I_0\cos^2\theta$. Brewster's angle: $\tan\theta_B = n_2/n_1$.
