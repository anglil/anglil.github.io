Chapter 3Energy and Work

Energy methods provide a scalar alternative to Newton's vector equations. In many competition problems — especially those involving constraints, curved paths, or unknown forces — energy conservation is the fastest route to the answer.

3.1 Work Done by a Force

Definition 3.1 — Work

The work done by a force $\mathbf{F}$ on a particle moving along a path $\mathcal{C}$ is

$$W = \int_{\mathcal{C}} \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r}.$$

For a constant force: $W = \mathbf{F}\cdot\Delta\mathbf{r} = F\,\Delta r\,\cos\theta$.

3.2 Work–Energy Theorem

Theorem 3.1 — Work–Energy Theorem

The net work done on a particle equals the change in its kinetic energy:

$$W_{\text{net}} = \Delta K = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2.$$
Proof

From $\mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}$: $W = \int \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r} = m\int \mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{v}\,dt = m\int \frac{d}{dt}\!\left(\frac{v^2}{2}\right)dt = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$. $\blacksquare$

3.3 Conservative Forces and Potential Energy

Definition 3.2 — Conservative Force

A force $\mathbf{F}$ is conservative if the work it does depends only on the endpoints, not the path. Equivalently, $\oint \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r} = 0$ for every closed loop, or $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$.

For a conservative force there exists a scalar function $U(\mathbf{r})$ (potential energy) such that $\mathbf{F} = -\nabla U$ and $W_{A\to B} = U(A) - U(B) = -\Delta U$.

3.4 Conservation of Mechanical Energy

Theorem 3.2 — Conservation of Energy

When only conservative forces do work:

$$K_i + U_i = K_f + U_f \qquad\text{or equivalently}\qquad E = K + U = \text{const}.$$

When non-conservative forces (friction, drag) also act: $\Delta E = W_{\text{nc}}$.

Example 3.1 — Loop-the-Loop

A small ball starts from rest at height $h$ above the bottom of a frictionless circular loop of radius $R$. Find the minimum $h$ so the ball completes the loop.

Solution

At the top of the loop, the ball needs $mg \leq mv_{\text{top}}^2/R$, so $v_{\text{top}}^2 \geq gR$.

Energy conservation from start to top: $mgh = mg(2R) + \frac{1}{2}mv_{\text{top}}^2$.

Setting $v_{\text{top}}^2 = gR$: $gh = 2gR + gR/2$, so $h = \frac{5}{2}R$.

3.5 Power

Definition 3.3 — Power
$$P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \mathbf{F}\cdot\mathbf{v}.$$

3.6 Potential Energy Diagrams

Plotting $U(x)$ versus $x$ gives deep insight into the qualitative behavior of one-dimensional motion. The particle moves in the region where $E \geq U(x)$. Turning points occur where $E = U(x)$. Equilibria occur at $dU/dx = 0$: stable if $d^2U/dx^2 > 0$, unstable if $d^2U/dx^2 < 0$.

Example 3.2 — Lennard-Jones Potential

The interaction between two neutral atoms is modeled by $U(r) = \epsilon\!\left[\left(\frac{r_0}{r}\right)^{12} - 2\left(\frac{r_0}{r}\right)^6\right]$. Find the equilibrium separation and the depth of the potential well.

Solution

$dU/dr = 0 \implies -12\,\frac{r_0^{12}}{r^{13}} + 12\,\frac{r_0^6}{r^7} = 0 \implies r^6 = r_0^6 \implies r = r_0$.

$U(r_0) = \epsilon(1 - 2) = -\epsilon$, so the well depth is $\epsilon$.

3.7 Gravitational Potential Energy (General)

Theorem 3.3 — Escape Velocity

The minimum launch speed from the surface of a body of mass $M$ and radius $R$ (ignoring atmosphere) is

$$v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}.$$

3.8 Exercises

Exercise 3.1

A block of mass $m$ slides down a quarter-circle ramp of radius $R$ starting from rest, then across a rough horizontal surface (coefficient $\mu_k$). How far does it travel before stopping?

Exercise 3.2

A particle moves under the potential $U(x) = U_0\cosh(x/a)$. Find the period of small oscillations about the equilibrium.

Exercise 3.3

A variable-mass chain of total length $L$ and mass $M$ hangs from a table edge with length $x_0$ initially hanging. Released from rest, find the speed when the last link leaves the table (frictionless).

Exercise 3.4

A rocket of initial mass $M_0$ expels fuel at rate $\dot{m}$ with exhaust velocity $v_e$ relative to the rocket. Using energy considerations, verify the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation $\Delta v = v_e \ln(M_0/M_f)$.
