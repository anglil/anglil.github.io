Chapter 7Oscillations and Waves

7.1 Simple Harmonic Motion

Definition 7.1 — SHM

A system undergoes simple harmonic motion if the restoring force is proportional to displacement: $F = -kx$. The equation of motion is

$$\ddot{x} + \omega^2 x = 0, \qquad \omega = \sqrt{k/m}.$$

General solution: $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$.

Period $T = 2\pi/\omega$. Energy: $E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2 = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 A^2$.

SystemPeriod $T$
Mass–spring$2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$
Simple pendulum (small angle)$2\pi\sqrt{\ell/g}$
Physical pendulum$2\pi\sqrt{I/(mgh)}$
Torsional oscillator$2\pi\sqrt{I/\kappa}$
Example 7.1 — Mass on Two Springs

A mass $m$ is attached between two springs of constants $k_1$ and $k_2$ in series (end to end). Find the effective spring constant and the period.

Solution

For springs in series: $1/k_{\text{eff}} = 1/k_1 + 1/k_2$, so $k_{\text{eff}} = k_1 k_2/(k_1+k_2)$.

$T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k_{\text{eff}}} = 2\pi\sqrt{m(k_1+k_2)/(k_1 k_2)}$.

7.2 Damped Oscillations

With a linear drag force $-b\dot{x}$: $m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = 0$. Defining $\gamma = b/(2m)$:

$$x(t) = A e^{-\gamma t}\cos(\omega_d t + \phi), \qquad \omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \gamma^2}.$$

Underdamped: $\gamma < \omega_0$. Critically damped: $\gamma = \omega_0$. Overdamped: $\gamma > \omega_0$.

7.3 Forced Oscillations and Resonance

Driven oscillator: $m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = F_0\cos(\omega_d t)$. Steady-state amplitude:

$$A(\omega_d) = \frac{F_0/m}{\sqrt{(\omega_0^2 - \omega_d^2)^2 + (2\gamma\omega_d)^2}}.$$

Resonance occurs at $\omega_d \approx \omega_0$ (exactly at $\omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - 2\gamma^2}$ for displacement). The quality factor $Q = \omega_0/(2\gamma)$ measures the sharpness of the resonance peak.

7.4 Mechanical Waves

Definition 7.2 — Wave Equation
$$\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2} = v^2\frac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2}$$

General solution: $y(x,t) = f(x - vt) + g(x + vt)$. Harmonic wave: $y = A\sin(kx - \omega t + \phi)$ with $v = \omega/k = \lambda f$.

Transverse wave on a string: $v = \sqrt{T/\mu}$ where $T$ is tension, $\mu$ is linear mass density.

Sound wave in a gas: $v = \sqrt{\gamma P/\rho} = \sqrt{\gamma R T / M}$.

7.5 Superposition and Standing Waves

Two waves of equal amplitude traveling in opposite directions produce a standing wave:

$$y = 2A\sin(kx)\cos(\omega t).$$

Fixed-end string (length $L$): $\lambda_n = 2L/n$, $f_n = nv/(2L)$, $n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$

Open pipe: same harmonics. Closed pipe: $f_n = nv/(4L)$, $n = 1, 3, 5, \ldots$ (odd harmonics only).

7.6 Beats and the Doppler Effect

Beats: $f_{\text{beat}} = |f_1 - f_2|$.

Theorem 7.1 — Doppler Effect
$$f' = f\,\frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s}$$

Upper sign when source/observer approach; lower when they recede. $v$ = wave speed, $v_o$ = observer speed, $v_s$ = source speed.
