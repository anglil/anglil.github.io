Chapter 13Special Relativity and Modern Physics

13.1 Postulates of Special Relativity

Einstein's Postulates
  1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames.
  2. The speed of light in vacuum $c = 3\times10^8\,\text{m/s}$ is the same in all inertial frames.

13.2 Lorentz Transformations

For frame $S'$ moving at $v$ along $x$ relative to $S$, with $\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}$:

$$x' = \gamma(x-vt), \quad t' = \gamma(t-vx/c^2), \quad y'=y, \quad z'=z.$$

13.3 Time Dilation and Length Contraction

Theorem 13.1
$$\Delta t = \gamma\,\Delta t_0 \qquad\text{(time dilation)},$$$$L = L_0/\gamma \qquad\text{(length contraction along the direction of motion)}.$$
Example 13.1 — Muon Lifetime

Muons created in the upper atmosphere at $v = 0.998c$ have a proper lifetime $\tau_0 = 2.2\,\mu\text{s}$. How far do they travel in the Earth's frame?

Solution

$\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1-0.998^2} \approx 15.8$. In the Earth frame, $\tau = \gamma\tau_0 = 34.8\,\mu\text{s}$. Distance: $d = v\tau = 0.998(3\times10^8)(34.8\times10^{-6}) \approx 10.4\,\text{km}$.

13.4 Relativistic Velocity Addition

$$u' = \frac{u-v}{1-uv/c^2}.$$

13.5 Relativistic Momentum and Energy

Theorem 13.2 — Energy–Momentum Relation
$$\mathbf{p} = \gamma m\mathbf{v}, \qquad E = \gamma mc^2, \qquad E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2.$$

Kinetic energy: $K = (\gamma-1)mc^2$. Rest energy: $E_0 = mc^2$.

For massless particles (photons): $E = pc$, $p = E/c = h/\lambda$.

13.6 Photoelectric Effect

Einstein's Photoelectric Equation
$$K_{\max} = hf - \phi$$

where $h = 6.626\times10^{-34}\,\text{J·s}$ and $\phi$ is the work function. Threshold frequency: $f_0 = \phi/h$.

13.7 Bohr Model of the Hydrogen Atom

Theorem 13.3 — Bohr Energy Levels
$$E_n = -\frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2}, \qquad r_n = n^2 a_0, \qquad a_0 = 0.529\,\text{Å}.$$

Transition wavelengths: $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H\left(\frac{1}{n_f^2}-\frac{1}{n_i^2}\right)$ with $R_H = 1.097\times10^7\,\text{m}^{-1}$.

13.8 de Broglie Wavelength and Wave–Particle Duality

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv} \qquad\text{(non-relativistic)}.$$

13.9 Nuclear Physics Basics

Mass–energy equivalence: Binding energy $B = [Z m_p + N m_n - M_{\text{nucleus}}]c^2$.

Radioactive decay: $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$, half-life $t_{1/2} = \ln 2/\lambda$.

Decay types: $\alpha$ ($^4_2$He emission), $\beta^-$ ($n\to p + e^- + \bar\nu$), $\beta^+$ ($p\to n + e^+ + \nu$), $\gamma$ (photon emission).

13.10 Exercises

Exercise 13.1

A spaceship travels at $0.6c$ relative to Earth. It fires a probe forward at $0.8c$ relative to the ship. What is the probe's speed relative to Earth?

Exercise 13.2

A photon of wavelength $\lambda$ Compton-scatters off a stationary electron. Show that the wavelength shift is $\Delta\lambda = (h/m_e c)(1-\cos\theta)$. Find the maximum energy transferred to the electron for $\lambda = 0.05\,\text{nm}$.

Exercise 13.3

The sun's luminosity is $L = 3.846\times10^{26}\,\text{W}$. Estimate how much mass the sun loses per second due to nuclear fusion.

Exercise 13.4

An electron is confined in a one-dimensional box of length $L$. Using the de Broglie condition, find the allowed energy levels. Compare with $E_n = n^2\pi^2\hbar^2/(2mL^2)$.
