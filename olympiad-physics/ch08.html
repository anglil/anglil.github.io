Chapter 8Thermodynamics and Kinetic Theory

8.1 Temperature and the Zeroth Law

The zeroth law establishes thermal equilibrium as an equivalence relation: if $A$ is in equilibrium with $C$ and $B$ is in equilibrium with $C$, then $A$ is in equilibrium with $B$. This justifies the existence of temperature as a state function.

8.2 Ideal Gas Law

Theorem 8.1 — Ideal Gas Law
$$PV = nRT = Nk_BT$$

where $R = 8.314\,\text{J/(mol·K)}$ and $k_B = R/N_A = 1.381\times 10^{-23}\,\text{J/K}$.

8.3 Kinetic Theory of Gases

Theorem 8.2 — Pressure from Kinetic Theory
$$P = \frac{1}{3}\rho\,\overline{v^2} = \frac{1}{3}\frac{N}{V}m\overline{v^2}.$$

Average kinetic energy per molecule: $\frac{1}{2}m\overline{v^2} = \frac{3}{2}k_B T$. Root-mean-square speed: $v_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{3k_BT/m}$.

Equipartition theorem: Each quadratic degree of freedom contributes $\frac{1}{2}k_B T$ to the average energy. For an ideal gas with $f$ degrees of freedom: $U = \frac{f}{2}nRT$. Monoatomic: $f=3$. Diatomic (rigid): $f=5$.

8.4 First Law of Thermodynamics

Theorem 8.3 — First Law
$$\Delta U = Q - W$$

where $Q$ is heat added to the system and $W = \int P\,dV$ is work done by the system.

ProcessConstraint$W$$\Delta U$$Q$
Isochoric$V=\text{const}$$0$$nC_v\Delta T$$nC_v\Delta T$
Isobaric$P=\text{const}$$P\Delta V$$nC_v\Delta T$$nC_p\Delta T$
Isothermal$T=\text{const}$$nRT\ln(V_f/V_i)$$0$$W$
Adiabatic$Q=0$$-\Delta U$$nC_v\Delta T$$0$

For an adiabatic process: $PV^\gamma = \text{const}$, $TV^{\gamma-1} = \text{const}$, where $\gamma = C_p/C_v = (f+2)/f$.

8.5 Heat Engines and the Second Law

Theorem 8.4 — Carnot Efficiency

The maximum efficiency of a heat engine operating between temperatures $T_H$ and $T_C$:

$$\eta_{\text{Carnot}} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}.$$
Definition 8.1 — Entropy
$$dS = \frac{\delta Q_{\text{rev}}}{T}, \qquad \Delta S \geq 0 \;\text{(isolated system)}.$$
Example 8.1 — Otto Cycle

An ideal Otto cycle operates between volumes $V_1$ (max) and $V_2$ (min) with compression ratio $r = V_1/V_2$. Find the efficiency.

Solution

The Otto cycle has two adiabats and two isochors. $\eta = 1 - 1/r^{\gamma-1}$.

8.6 Heat Transfer

Conduction: $\dot{Q} = -\kappa A\,(dT/dx)$ (Fourier's law).

Radiation: $P = \varepsilon\sigma A T^4$ (Stefan–Boltzmann law).

8.7 Exercises

Exercise 8.1

An ideal monoatomic gas undergoes a cycle: isothermal expansion at $T_1$ from $V_1$ to $2V_1$, isochoric cooling to $T_2$, isothermal compression at $T_2$ back to $V_1$, isochoric heating to $T_1$. Find the efficiency.

Exercise 8.2

Two identical blocks at temperatures $T_1$ and $T_2$ (with $T_1 > T_2$) are brought into thermal contact. Find the entropy change of the universe when they reach equilibrium.

Exercise 8.3

A gas satisfies the van der Waals equation $(P + a/V^2)(V - b) = RT$ (for one mole). Find the work done in an isothermal expansion from $V_1$ to $V_2$.
