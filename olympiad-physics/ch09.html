Chapter 9Electrostatics

9.1 Coulomb's Law

Theorem 9.1 — Coulomb's Law
$$\mathbf{F}_{12} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}\,\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12}$$

where $\varepsilon_0 = 8.854\times10^{-12}\,\text{F/m}$ and $k = 1/(4\pi\varepsilon_0) = 8.99\times10^9\,\text{N·m}^2/\text{C}^2$.

9.2 Electric Field

Definition 9.1 — Electric Field
$$\mathbf{E}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\sum_i \frac{q_i}{|\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_i|^2}\,\hat{\mathbf{r}}_i \qquad\text{or}\qquad \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\int\frac{dq}{r^2}\,\hat{\mathbf{r}}.$$
Example 9.1 — Field of a Uniform Ring

Find the electric field on the axis of a uniformly charged ring of radius $R$ and total charge $Q$.

Solution

By symmetry, only the axial component survives. At distance $z$ along the axis:

$$E_z = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{Qz}{(R^2+z^2)^{3/2}}.$$

9.3 Gauss's Law

Theorem 9.2 — Gauss's Law
$$\oint_S \mathbf{E}\cdot d\mathbf{A} = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}.$$

Standard applications: spherical symmetry (point charge, sphere), cylindrical symmetry (infinite wire, cylinder), planar symmetry (infinite sheet). For an infinite plane of surface charge density $\sigma$: $E = \sigma/(2\varepsilon_0)$.

9.4 Electric Potential

Definition 9.2 — Electric Potential
$$V(\mathbf{r}) = -\int_{\text{ref}}^{\mathbf{r}} \mathbf{E}\cdot d\boldsymbol{\ell}, \qquad \mathbf{E} = -\nabla V.$$

Point charge: $V = kq/r$.

9.5 Capacitance

Definition 9.3 — Capacitance
$$C = \frac{Q}{V}.$$
Geometry$C$
Parallel plates (area $A$, gap $d$)$\varepsilon_0 A/d$
Coaxial cylinders (radii $a,b$, length $L$)$2\pi\varepsilon_0 L/\ln(b/a)$
Concentric spheres (radii $a,b$)$4\pi\varepsilon_0 ab/(b-a)$
Isolated sphere (radius $R$)$4\pi\varepsilon_0 R$

Energy stored: $U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{Q^2}{2C} = \frac{1}{2}QV$. Energy density: $u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$.

9.6 Dielectrics

A dielectric of constant $\kappa$ reduces the field: $C \to \kappa C$. $\mathbf{D} = \varepsilon_0\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{P} = \varepsilon\mathbf{E}$ where $\varepsilon = \kappa\varepsilon_0$.

9.7 Method of Images

For a point charge $q$ at distance $d$ from an infinite grounded conducting plane, replace the plane with an image charge $-q$ at distance $d$ on the other side. The force on $q$: $F = kq^2/(2d)^2$.

9.8 Exercises

Exercise 9.1

Find the electric field at the center of a uniformly charged semicircular arc of radius $R$ and total charge $Q$.

Exercise 9.2

A conducting sphere of radius $R$ carries charge $Q$. It is surrounded by a concentric conducting shell of inner radius $2R$ and outer radius $3R$ carrying charge $-Q$. Find $E(r)$ and $V(r)$ everywhere.

Exercise 9.3

Two large parallel plates carry surface charge densities $+\sigma$ and $-\sigma$. A dielectric slab of thickness $t < d$ and constant $\kappa$ is inserted between them. Find the new field and the capacitance.

Exercise 9.4

A dipole $\mathbf{p} = p\,\hat{\mathbf{z}}$ is at the origin. Find the potential and field at $(r,\theta)$ for $r \gg$ dipole size. Verify $\mathbf{E} = -\nabla V$.
