Chapter 10DC Circuits

10.1 Current and Resistance

Definition 10.1
$$I = \frac{dQ}{dt}, \qquad \mathbf{J} = nq\mathbf{v}_d, \qquad R = \frac{\rho L}{A}.$$
Ohm's Law
$$V = IR.$$

Power dissipated: $P = IV = I^2R = V^2/R$.

10.2 Resistor Networks

Series: $R_{\text{eq}} = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots$ (same current).

Parallel: $1/R_{\text{eq}} = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2 + \cdots$ (same voltage).

Example 10.1 — Wheatstone Bridge

Four resistors $R_1, R_2, R_3, R_4$ form a bridge with a galvanometer across the middle. Show the bridge is balanced (no current through the galvanometer) when $R_1/R_2 = R_3/R_4$.

Solution

At balance, the potential at the two midpoints is equal. Let current $I_1$ flow through $R_1, R_2$ and $I_2$ through $R_3, R_4$. At balance: $I_1 R_1 = I_2 R_3$ and $I_1 R_2 = I_2 R_4$. Dividing: $R_1/R_2 = R_3/R_4$.

10.3 Kirchhoff's Laws

Kirchhoff's Junction Rule (KCL)

$\sum I_{\text{in}} = \sum I_{\text{out}}$ at every node.

Kirchhoff's Loop Rule (KVL)

$\sum \Delta V = 0$ around every closed loop.

10.4 EMF and Internal Resistance

A real battery: $\mathcal{E} = V_{\text{terminal}} + Ir$, where $r$ is internal resistance. Maximum power transfer to an external load $R$: $R = r$.

10.5 RC Circuits

Theorem 10.1 — RC Charging and Discharging

Charging: $q(t) = C\mathcal{E}(1-e^{-t/\tau})$, $I(t) = (\mathcal{E}/R)e^{-t/\tau}$.

Discharging: $q(t) = Q_0 e^{-t/\tau}$, $I(t) = -(Q_0/\tau)e^{-t/\tau}$.

Time constant $\tau = RC$.

10.6 Thévenin and Norton Equivalents

Any linear two-terminal network can be replaced by a voltage source $V_{\text{Th}}$ in series with $R_{\text{Th}}$ (Thévenin), or a current source $I_N = V_{\text{Th}}/R_{\text{Th}}$ in parallel with $R_{\text{Th}}$ (Norton).

10.7 Exercises

Exercise 10.2

Exercise 10.3
